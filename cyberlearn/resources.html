Common signs of an online scam

Warning signs to look for in emails, messages, and phone calls.

Warning signs:

  • Urgency: "Act now!", "Limited time!", "Account will be closed"
  • Personal information requests: Banks never ask for passwords or PINs
  • Too good to be true: Unexpected prizes or deals
  • Poor spelling: Real companies proofread their messages
  • Suspicious links: Check where links actually go before clicking

What to do:

  • • Don't click links or download attachments
  • • Contact companies directly using trusted phone numbers
  • • Report suspicious emails to report@phishing.gov.uk
  • • Forward suspicious texts to 7726 (free)

Making strong passwords

Create secure passwords that are easy to remember.

The 3-word method:

Pick three random, unrelated words and combine them with numbers and symbols.

Good example

GardenBlueMoon42!

Bad example

password123

Memory tricks:

  • Sentence method: "I love walking at 7am" → ILoveWalking@7am!
  • Place + object: Think of a place and object → BeachUmbrella89
  • Never use: Birthdays, names, common words, or the same password twice

Staying safe on public Wi-Fi

What's safe and what's not when using coffee shop or airport Wi-Fi.

Public Wi-Fi networks are not encrypted, which means other people can potentially see what you're doing.

Safe on public Wi-Fi:

  • • Reading news websites
  • • Checking the weather
  • • Browsing social media
  • • Looking at restaurant menus

Not safe on public Wi-Fi:

  • • Online banking
  • • Shopping with card details
  • • Entering passwords
  • • Accessing sensitive data

Best practice: Wait until you're home on your own Wi-Fi for anything involving passwords or payments. If urgent, use your mobile data (4G/5G) instead.

Common terms

Phishing
Fake emails or messages designed to trick you into giving away personal information.
Vishing
"Voice phishing" - scam phone calls pretending to be from your bank or trusted organizations.
Smishing
"SMS phishing" - scam text messages like fake delivery notifications.
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Extra security requiring both your password and another proof (like a code sent to your phone).
HTTPS
The "s" stands for "secure". Look for this and a padlock icon before entering personal information.

Need help?

Report a scam

  • Suspicious emails: report@phishing.gov.uk
  • Suspicious texts: Forward to 7726 (free)
  • Report scams: Action Fraud 0300 123 2040

Get support

  • Contact your bank immediately if scammed
  • Ask a trusted family member or friend
  • Visit Age UK for more resources
