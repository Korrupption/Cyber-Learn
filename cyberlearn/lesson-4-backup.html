Lesson 4

Phone and text scams

Scammers use phone calls (vishing) and text messages (smishing) to pressure you into acting quickly. They often pretend to be your bank, the police, or a delivery service.

Scenario question

A caller says they are from your bank's fraud team.

They claim your account is at risk and ask you to move your savings to a "safe" account.

Spotting phone scams

  • They rush you: "act now or your money is gone"
  • They ask for full PIN, passwords, or card numbers
  • They want you to lie to bank staff or go to a branch
  • They send a courier to collect your bank card

Recognising text scams

  • "Royal Mail" asking for a delivery fee via link
  • "HMRC" saying you are owed a tax refund
  • Messages from a "new number" claiming to be family
  • Links that ask for card details on unfamiliar sites
